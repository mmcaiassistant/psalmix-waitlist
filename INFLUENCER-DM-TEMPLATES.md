# Influencer DM Templates for PsalMix Waitlist

**Target Audiences:**
1. LDS Mom Influencers
2. Christian Family Influencers
3. Homeschool Influencers
4. Clean Living Influencers

**Approach:** Authentic, personal, not salesy. Lead with shared values, not product pitch.

---

## General Outreach Strategy

### Before You DM:
- [ ] Follow them (if you don't already)
- [ ] Engage with 2-3 recent posts (genuine comments, not generic)
- [ ] Check if they've posted about similar topics (family values, clean media, parenting struggles)
- [ ] Personalize every message (reference specific post, shared value, or mutual connection)

### Timing:
- **Best times to send:** Tue-Thu, 9-11am or 7-9pm (when moms are checking phones)
- **Avoid:** Weekends, early mornings, late nights

### Follow-Up Protocol:
- Wait 48-72 hours before following up
- If no response after 2nd message, let it go gracefully
- Never be pushy or guilt-trippy

## Template 1: Cold Outreach - Personal Connection

**Use when:** You have a genuine connection (similar values, audience overlap, or specific post resonates)

---

**Subject/Opening:**
> Hi [Name]! I've been following you for a while and love your heart for [specific thing - homeschooling/faith/family/etc].

**Body:**
> I'm reaching out because I just launched something I think might resonate with you (and your community).
> 
> I'm McKinzie, mom of 3 and homeschooler. A while back, I had one of those moments where Spotify's "clean" filter failed me big time—inappropriate lyrics came on during a family car ride, and I felt like I'd failed my kids.
> 
> So I built PsalMix—a music streaming app where every song is vetted by real parents. No surprise explicit content. Music that actually aligns with family values.
> 
> I just opened the waitlist today, and I'd love for you to check it out: [link]
> 
> No pressure at all, but if this resonates with you, I'd be honored if you shared it with your community. Either way, thanks for all you do to encourage families like mine. 💜

**Sign-off:**
> — McKinzie

---

**Why This Works:**
- Starts with genuine appreciation
- Tells a relatable story (not a sales pitch)
- Makes a clear ask without being pushy
- Gives them an easy out ("no pressure")

## DM Etiquette Reminders

### DO:
✅ Personalize every message (use their name, reference specific content)  
✅ Lead with empathy and shared values  
✅ Make it easy to say yes OR no  
✅ Offer value (not just "promote my thing")  
✅ Follow up once, then gracefully let go  
✅ Thank anyone who shares, immediately and sincerely  

### DON'T:
❌ Copy-paste generic messages  
❌ Be pushy or entitled  
❌ Guilt-trip ("I thought you'd care about this")  
❌ Follow up more than twice  
❌ Make it about you (make it about shared mission)  
❌ Assume they owe you anything

## Quick Win List: Start Here

**Tier 1: People You Already Know**
- Friends who are influencers (even micro-influencers)
- Mutual connections (ask for intros)
- People who've engaged with your content before

**Tier 2: Micro-Influencers (5K-50K)**
- High engagement
- Easier to reach
- More likely to respond personally

**Tier 3: Mid-Tier (50K-200K)**
- Reach out with personal connection angle
- Reference specific content they've posted
- Offer early partnership perks

**Tier 4: Large Accounts (200K+)**
- Lower response rate, but high impact if they share
- Need strong hook (mutual connection, press coverage, social proof)
- Consider cold email to their business email instead of DM

---

**Remember:** Every "no" is one step closer to a "yes." Not everyone will resonate with PsalMix, and that's okay. Focus on finding your people—the ones who share your values and mission. Those are the partnerships that will matter most. 💜🎵
