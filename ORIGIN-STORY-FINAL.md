# Why I Built PsalMix: A Mom's Story

I'll never forget the moment.

We were driving to park day—windows down, everyone in good spirits. My kids had been begging to listen to music, so I pulled up Spotify and turned on the "clean" filter. *Problem solved*, I thought.

Three songs in, my 8-year-old asked, "Mom, what does that word mean?"

My stomach dropped. The "clean" version still had lyrics I would *never* say in front of my kids. Words that were technically not profanity, but absolutely not family-friendly. I fumbled for my phone to skip the song, but the damage was done. The car went quiet. The mood shifted.

That drive home, I felt like I'd failed them.

## The Problem I Couldn't Ignore

I'm McKinzie—mom of three, homeschooling, Latter-day Saint, and a lifelong music lover. Music has always been part of our home. We dance in the kitchen, sing during chores, and use playlists to set the mood for everything from morning routine to family game night.

But after that car ride, I realized: **the tools we have aren't built for families like ours.**

Spotify's "clean" filter is a checkbox, not a promise. YouTube Music is a minefield of suggestions. Even kid-focused apps are either too babyish for older kids or still slip through questionable content.

I started manually vetting every song. Reading lyrics. Checking reviews. Building playlists one painstaking track at a time. It took *hours*—and even then, I'd miss things.

## What If There Was a Better Way?

I kept thinking: *There has to be parents like me all over the world doing this same tedious work. What if we pooled our efforts? What if there was a place where music was actually, genuinely family-safe—not just "profanity-free"?*

That's when the idea for **PsalMix** was born.

I wanted a music platform where:
- Every song is reviewed by *real parents* for lyrical content, themes, and age-appropriateness
- Families can filter by values (not just "clean" vs. explicit)
- Playlists are curated with intention—uplifting, fun, peaceful, energizing
- Kids can explore music safely, without me hovering over their shoulder
- Parents can trust what's playing, even when we're not in the room

## Building It for My Kids First

I started small. I built a prototype just for my own family—a simple app with hand-curated playlists. Songs we loved. Music that reflected our values. No surprises.

My kids loved it. They started requesting "Mom's music app" instead of Spotify. They'd make their own playlists and share them with each other. For the first time in a long time, I could hit "play" without anxiety.

But then something unexpected happened.

## The Moment I Realized This Was Bigger

I shared a screenshot of the app in a homeschool Facebook group, just casually mentioning I'd built something for my family. Within an hour, I had 40+ messages from moms asking, *"Can I use this? When can I get access? I've been looking for this exact thing!"*

That's when I knew: **this isn't just my problem. This is a gap affecting millions of families.**

So I made the decision to turn PsalMix into something bigger—a real platform, built for all of us who want music to be a *positive force* in our homes, not a source of stress.

## What PsalMix Is (and Isn't)

PsalMix isn't about sheltering kids from the world. It's not about judgment or fear.

It's about **intentionality**. It's about reclaiming music as something that *adds* to your family culture instead of undermining it.

It's a tool for parents who:
- Care about the messages their kids absorb
- Want music that aligns with their values (faith-based, clean, uplifting, wholesome)
- Are tired of manually vetting every single song
- Want their kids to discover new music *safely*

Whether you're LDS, Christian, secular-but-values-driven, or just a parent who wants less stress around screen time—PsalMix is for you.
